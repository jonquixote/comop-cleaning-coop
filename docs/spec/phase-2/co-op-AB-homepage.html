    Why worker-owned

    A cleaning company keeps the difference between what you pay and what the cleaner earns. A gig app keeps it and adds a stranger every visit. We removed that layer entirely: the people who clean, drive, and cook own the company, vote on its rules, and split its surplus. Turnover in worker co-ops runs at roughly half the industry rate — which is the real reason you get the same trusted person at your door, visit after visit.

    Questions, answered straight
    Is the deposit actually refundable?
    Yes — in full, any time before your first visit, no reason needed. One text. If we don't launch in your neighborhood, it comes back automatically.
    Who exactly shows up?
    A worker-owner of the cooperative — Live Scan background-checked, bonded, and insured before any first visit. For household roles involving children: TrustLine-registered and pediatric-CPR certified. You meet your person before the first visit, and the same person returns each time, with a vetted co-op backup when she's sick — so a sick day never strands you.
    Are you a licensed company?
    We are organizing as a California worker cooperative corporation — the legal form built for businesses owned by their workers. General liability insurance, fidelity bonding, and workers' compensation are in place before any first visit. We'll show you the certificates; ask.
    Why do you cost more than a cash cleaner?
    Cash prices are usually subsidized by no insurance, no taxes, and no protection for anyone — including you. Our price carries real wages, real workers' comp, bonding, and liability coverage. The table above shows every dollar. What we cost less than: the big cleaning brands, and nanny agencies charging thousands in placement fees.
