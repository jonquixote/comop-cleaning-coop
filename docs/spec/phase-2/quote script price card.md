# Quote Script + Price Card
**One script, three settings: door, call, coffee. The goal of every conversation is a logged outcome — commit, qualified, or lead with an objection code. Never leave without the ask.**

---

## The script

**Open (warm):** "You know I've been building something — I want to actually quote you like a real customer, and I want your honest no if it's a no. Two minutes."
**Open (cold/door/reply-call):** "Hi, I'm Johnny — I'm founding a worker-owned cleaning and household co-op launching here on the Westside. Not selling hard; I'm quoting founding households this month and I'd rather hear a real no than a polite maybe."

**Qualify (four questions, in order):**
1. "Who handles your cleaning now — a company, an individual, or you?" *(sets the price anchor)*
2. "What breaks first — reliability, trust, or coverage when they're sick?" *(their pain names your pitch)*
3. "Kids at home? Ages? Who does school pickup?" *(opens Form B or closes it)*
4. "Roughly what does the current setup run a month?" *(they'll usually tell you)*

**The pitch (30 seconds, verbatim skeleton):**
"Here's the whole model. The cleaner owns the company — she's not my employee, she's my co-owner. That's why the same person actually shows up every time, and why there's a covered backup when she's sick instead of a cancellation. Everyone's fingerprint background-checked, bonded, and insured before a first visit. And we publish our math: of your $58 hour, $29.50 is her wage — about 51% above what the industry pays — and zero goes to an owner, because there isn't one."

**Form B probe (ask every household with kids, verbatim):**
"One more thing — the thing families actually ask us for. If one vetted, CPR-certified person could cover cleaning Tuesday morning *and* pickup Tuesday–Thursday afternoons — one person, one bill, backup included — what would that be worth to your week?"
*(Any real engagement with schedule + price + timing = log as QUALIFIED, Form B.)*

**The close (always, no exceptions):**
"Want me to hold a founding spot? It's $35, fully refundable any time before your first visit, and it comes off your first bill." — or for a verbal: "Can I put you down for a first clean the week of [date] at [price]?"
**Log the answer either way.** A no with a reason is data we paid for; get the objection code.

## Objection responses (honest ones only)

- **O1 "My cleaner charges $150 cash."** → "She's probably worth more than she's charging — cash prices usually mean no insurance, no taxes, no workers' comp, no protection for her *or* you if something breaks or someone gets hurt. Our price carries all of that, and the wage difference goes to her, not to an office. We're not trying to beat cash — we're the option for households that want it done right."
- **O2 "You're brand new."** → "Completely fair. That's what the deposit structure is for — $35, refundable on one text, and you meet your person before any first visit. We're asking for a coffee-sized bet, not trust we haven't earned."
- **O3 "We have someone we love."** → "Keep her — seriously. Two things: we have a backup bench for when she's away, and if she ever wants a job with a wage, insurance, and ownership, we're hiring. That's the whole point of this thing."
- **O4 "Bad timing."** → "The list is the timing fix — founding spot now, first visit whenever you're ready, refundable until then."
- **O5 Deposit balk.** → "No deposit needed to get your flat quote in writing — the deposit only holds a founding spot and founding pricing." *(Log as LEAD, O5 — never pressure past one sentence.)*

**Every tier:** Live Scan background check · bonded & insured before first visit · same person + vetted backup · flat pricing, published math · $35 refundable founding deposit, credited to first service.

**Floor discipline (from the protocol, non-negotiable):** no discounts in round one; nothing below $55/labor-hr (A) or the tiered rates (B) counts as a commit. The shareable price card *is* the landing page — text the link with `?v=a` or `?v=b`.

**Log after every conversation:** name · neighborhood · form · stage · price-floor Y/N · deposit Y/N · stranger Y/N · objection code · one line of notes. Same day, no exceptions.
