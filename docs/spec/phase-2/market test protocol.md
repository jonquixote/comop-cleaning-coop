# Market Test Protocol — Pre-Registered
**Westside Household Co-op · Hypothesis 6**
**Status: LOCKED upon Johnny's written sign-off. After Day 0, no threshold, definition, or gate in this document may be changed. The single exception is the one pre-registered extension in §7. Amendments after Day 0 are void.**

## 1. What is being tested

**H-DEMAND:** Westside LA households will pay company-tier prices — ≥$55/labor-hour for cleaning (Form A) or ≥$42/hour blended for a household retainer (Form B) — to a brand-new worker-owned entrant, on the strength of the trust stack (worker-owned, bonded, insured, TrustLine, CPR, backup bench) and transparent pricing.

**H-SUPPLY:** Experienced domestic workers will accept W-2 employment at $26–30/hr with full continuous days, payroll taxes withheld, in exchange for legal protections, stability, and ownership.

**Joint rule, verbatim and binding:** *A pass on one side and a kill on the other is a full kill for the thesis. No exceptions, no "well one side looked good so let's keep going."* A full kill requires a written post-mortem before any revival attempt, and any revival is a new pre-registered test, not a continuation.

## 2. Window and channels

- **Day 0** = the first outbound message, post, or traffic push on any channel, whichever comes first. Test ends 23:59 on **Day 14**.
- **C1 — Personal (Johnny):** warm intros ≤1 degree out, calls, coffees, doors. Relationship distance logged per contact (warm / cold).
- **C2 — Remote digital:** landing page (A/B), Nextdoor, Facebook groups, Reddit, other channels per the Outreach Playbook.
- **C3 — Supply:** worker-center org outreach (IDEPSCA, PWC, CDWC) + direct worker conversations.

## 3. Definitions (the whole test lives here)

**COMMIT (demand)** — exactly one of:
- (a) a **paid $35 deposit** through the landing page (Stripe dashboard is the sole source of truth for deposits), or
- (b) a **personal-channel verbal commit** containing ALL of: agreed service + agreed price at/above floor + a named start window + contact info exchanged. Logged same day.

**The deposit-not-email rule:** email captures, "sounds amazing, keep me posted" texts, unpaid reserve-clicks, and lead-form submissions are **LEADS, never COMMITs**. Interest is cheap to say and worthless to us; only costly signals count.

**PRICE FLOOR:** ≥$55/labor-hour effective (Form A) · ≥$42/hour blended (Form B, as tiered on the price card). **No discounts in round one.** A yes below floor is logged as a LEAD with objection code O1 — it is never a COMMIT. This rule exists so the goalposts cannot slide via "just this once" pricing.

**STRANGER:** any C2 contact, or a C1 contact with no prior personal relationship (cold). Warm commits are real but partially confounded by friendship; the stranger minimum controls for charity.

**QUALIFIED RETAINER CONVERSATION:** a Form B discussion covering schedule + price + start timing, regardless of close.

**WORKER CONVERSATION (supply):** ≥20 minutes with a person currently or recently doing paid domestic work (cleaning / childcare / care), covering: the W-2 offer at $26–30 with full continuous days, what withholding means for take-home, the hybrid schedule, and worker ownership. **Compensated $30 for their time** (budget $200). Verdict logged as take / maybe / no.

**REASON CODES (supply no's):** R1 cash-preference/net-pay · R2 work-authorization ineligibility · R3 distrust of employers/formality · R4 schedule mismatch · R5 wage insufficient · R6 other.
**Pre-registered diagnostic split:** R1/R3/R4/R5 are **merits rejections** (they test the offer). R2 is **eligibility, not merits** — if R2 dominates the no's (≥3 of 5), the verdict is INDETERMINATE-ELIGIBILITY: not a kill, but a mandatory workforce-eligibility contingency review (incl. the LLC pathway question from the Framework doc) and a recruiting-channel shift before re-test. This split is fixed now so it cannot become a post-hoc excuse.

**ORG ENGAGEMENT:** a substantive reply or call (not an auto-acknowledgment). **Silence-counts-as-data rule:** each org gets exactly 2 touches over 10 days. Three silent orgs = a logged negative finding about the partnership channel and a cap on supply-side confidence grade — **never a kill by itself**, and never a third touch inside the window.

## 4. Effort floors (a kill only counts if the test was actually run)

A KILL verdict is valid only if, by Day 14:
- **C1:** ≥10 delivered quotes/conversations with real household decision-makers.
- **C2:** ≥300 unique landing sessions across both variants AND ≥25 logged outreach touches (ISO replies, permitted posts, DMs).
- **C3:** ≥5 worker conversations AND 3 orgs touched twice each.

If floors are unmet, the verdict is **INCOMPLETE** — not kill, not pass — and reflects on our execution, not the thesis.

## 5. Demand gates (C1 + C2 pooled; logged separately)

- **PASS:** ≥5 COMMITs, of which ≥2 STRANGER, AND retainer evidence: ≥1 Form B COMMIT **or** ≥3 qualified retainer conversations.
- **MARGINAL:** 3–4 COMMITs → the single §7 extension.
- **KILL:** ≤2 COMMITs with all effort floors met.

**A/B learning outputs (informative, not gates):** per-variant session→lead→deposit rates; objection distribution. **Pre-registered product decision:** the launch flagship is whichever Form produced more COMMITs; Form B stays in the launch lineup only if the retainer-evidence line above was met.

## 6. Supply gates

- **PASS-A:** ≥3 of the first 5+ worker conversations end take-it / want-next-step (named individuals), AND ≥1 org engaged.
- **PASS-B:** same worker numbers, all orgs silent (thesis passes; partnership channel flagged weak).
- **KILL:** ≥4 of 5 reject **on merits** (R1/R3/R4/R5 dominant).
- **INDETERMINATE-ELIGIBILITY:** per the R2 rule in §3.

## 7. The single extension

If either side lands MARGINAL (or floors are within reach but unmet), one 7-day extension may be taken, once, with every threshold unchanged. It must be invoked in writing on Day 14. There is no second extension.

## 8. Verdict table

| Demand | Supply | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| PASS | PASS (A or B) | **GO** — first jobs proceed on texts + Stripe invoices; booking-loop build unlocks |
| KILL | anything | **FULL KILL** |
| anything | KILL | **FULL KILL** |
| MARGINAL / INCOMPLETE either side | — | one extension (§7), then forced verdict |
| PASS | INDETERMINATE-ELIGIBILITY | eligibility contingency review, recruit-channel shift, re-test supply only |

## 9. Claims discipline (rides with this protocol; violations invalidate the affected commits)

Nothing may be stated to any prospect that will not be true at time of service. Fixed language: the co-op is **"organizing as a California worker cooperative corporation."** Insurance, bonding, background checks: **"in place before your first visit"** — never claimed as present-tense facts before they are. Deposits: **"fully refundable any time before your first visit, credited to your first service."** No "licensed" claims beyond entity truth. The trust stack is a guarantee we are choosing to be held to, which is exactly why it can be said out loud.

## 10. Data handling

Every contact logged **same day** in the tracking workbook. Stripe dashboard = deposits truth. Screenshots of ISO posts and replies saved. Session counts entered into the GATES sheet from analytics each evening. The GATES sheet computes the verdict from the logs — the spreadsheet, not memory or mood, says pass or kill.
