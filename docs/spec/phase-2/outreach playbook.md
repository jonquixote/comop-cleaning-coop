# Outreach Playbook — Channel C2 (Remote + Digital)
**Companion to the Market Test Protocol. Every action here gets logged in CHANNELS same-day. Claims discipline (§9) applies verbatim to every template.**

**Honest capability note:** Nextdoor and Facebook are login-walled — the scour loop below runs on your accounts, ~15 min/day. What's already done for you: the target map (verified via public sources), the templates, the norms per channel, and the tracking instrument. Reddit and Craigslist are public; those I could and did scout directly.

---

## 1. The target map (verified 2026-07-02)

**Facebook groups — parents/local, Westside:**
- **Peachhead** — the OG LA parents community, founded 1997 in Venice, 10,000+ members, Westside-born. Highest-value single venue in LA for this exact buyer. Join personally; learn its recommendation-thread culture before posting anything.
- **Westside Moms (Los Angeles)** — geo-gated: must live in Brentwood, Santa Monica, or Pacific Palisades to join. If you don't qualify by address, this is a venue for a member ally or a founding customer to post a genuine recommendation later — do not fake residency.
- **Culver City Moms** and **Moms of Culver City** — two separate active groups; join both.
- **LA Mommies** + neighborhood variants — search FB for "Mar Vista parents," "Venice moms," "West LA parents," "Palisades moms"; join the 3–5 largest that accept you.
- **International MOMS Club** chapters: Santa Monica, Culver City, West LA — chapter newsletters sometimes accept local-business blurbs; ask the chapter coordinator.

**Nextdoor:** claim a free **Business Page** week one (posts reach nearby neighborhoods with limits). The higher-yield motion is personal: set saved searches / notifications for **"cleaner," "housekeeper," "house cleaning," "nanny," "babysitter," "after school"** and answer ISO posts as a neighbor-founder within 2 hours. Recommendation threads ("anyone know a good…") are the single best demand surface on the platform.

**Reddit (public — no login wall for reading):** r/SantaMonica, r/LosAngeles (self-promo lives in the weekly threads — read each sub's rules first), r/CulverCity, r/venice. The founder-story post (T5) performs on Reddit precisely because it isn't corporate; naked ads get removed.

**Craigslist:** household-services ads are noise, but **ISO posts in "domestic gigs" and "household services wanted"** appear weekly — scan Westside listings daily, 2 minutes.

**Supply-side note (C3 support):** Care.com / UrbanSitter profiles are where individual workers pool — a recruiting surface for later, not a demand channel now.

## 2. The ranked backlog (start only after the loop above is running)

1. **Google Business Profile** — create "opening soon" week one. This is trust infrastructure, not marketing; it must exist before a single review can.
2. **Local press pitch** — Westside Current, Santa Monica Daily Press, The Palisadian-Post. The story is real news: *"LA cleaners are becoming owners — a worker co-op where the cleaner earns 51% above industry average, and the price sheet shows every dollar."* You're the media professional; one paragraph from you beats ten from me. Earned media is a free demand test.
3. **Farmers markets** — Mar Vista (Sunday), Santa Monica (Wednesday), Brentwood: table or flyer+QR, ~$50–100. Weekend 2 if the loop is healthy.
4. **PTA / school newsletters** — $50–150 sponsorships; the retainer's exact buyer reads these. Requires the entity to look real first.
5. **Buy Nothing groups** — commerce is banned; do not post. Only permissible surface: answering a direct ISO if rules allow "personal recommendations incl. self" — usually they don't. Skip unless certain.
6. **DSA-LA / LA co-op ecosystem** — announcement, not sales; reaches sympathizers and possible worker referrals, not Westside buyers.

## 3. Templates (edit voice, never claims)

**T1 — ISO reply (Nextdoor/FB, the workhorse):**
> Hi [name] — I'm a local and I'm founding something that might fit: a worker-owned cleaning & household co-op launching on the Westside. The cleaners literally own the company (it's why the same person actually stays), everyone's background-checked, bonded and insured before any first visit, and our price sheet shows where every dollar goes — including the $0 that goes to an owner. Happy to text you a flat quote if you tell me beds/baths. No pressure either way — and if you just want the price sheet: [link]

**T2 — Founder post (groups that allow intros/vendor threads):**
> Neighbors — I grew up here, and like a lot of you I grew up with a woman who cleaned our house, picked us up from school, and started dinner. She held our household together and never had a single legal protection. I'm founding a worker-owned co-op to make that exact job legal, insured, and owned by the workers themselves: cleaning from $175/visit, or one trusted household person on a weekly retainer — TrustLine-registered, CPR-certified, with a covered backup. Founding spots are limited so routes stay tight: [link]. Ask me anything, including the hard questions.

**T3 — DM follow-up (after any reply):** quote in one text: service, flat price, earliest start window, deposit link. No paragraphs.

**T4 — Vendor-day blurb (75 words max):** compress T2; lead with "the cleaner owns the company."

**T5 — Reddit founder post (weekly thread or where rules allow):** T2's story + one honest line: *"I'm pre-registering my success criteria publicly — if Westside households won't pay fair-wage prices, I'd rather find out in two weeks than two years."* Reddit rewards exactly this.

## 4. The daily loop (15 minutes, evenings)

1. Nextdoor saved-search alerts → answer new ISO posts with T1 (≤2 hrs old gets priority).
2. FB group search: "cleaner," "nanny," "housekeeper" sorted by New across joined groups → T1 where rules allow, DM where they don't.
3. Reddit + Craigslist scan (public, 3 min).
4. Log every touch in CHANNELS; enter the day's session count in GATES B4.
5. Any reply → T3 within the hour. Speed is the conversion variable you control.

**Norms rule, absolute:** read each group's pinned rules before first post; vendor threads and ISO replies only where permitted; one founder post per group per test window; never argue with a moderator; a ban costs the channel for good.
