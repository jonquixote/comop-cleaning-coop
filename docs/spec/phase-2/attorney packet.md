## B. The five questions (written opinions requested)

**Q1 — Governing wage order.** For hybrid domestic workers (substantial cleaning + childcare + cooking, therefore failing the ≤20% non-care threshold for "personal attendant" status) employed by a third-party cooperative and dispatched into private homes: does IWC **Wage Order 15** (household occupations) or **Wage Order 5** (public housekeeping) govern? DLSE's "Which IWC Order?" pamphlet routes non-householder-employed household workers to "some other order," while the IWC's personal-attendant expansion (DLSE Op. Ltr. 2005-11-23) shows deliberate third-party-employer coverage under Order 15 in the attendant context. Does the cooperative's planned future status as an HCO matter outside the personal-attendant context? *(Our defaults in A.4 are designed to comply under either order; we need the classification fixed for policy documents and any future audit.)*

**Q2 — Wage statements.** The compliant Labor Code **§226** itemization format for split-shift premium pay (earning code, rate shown, hours shown), and any pitfalls in our per-service hour-coded stub design.

**Q3 — CANRA status.** Please confirm our adopted reading that childcare-performing employees are **mandated reporters** under Penal Code **§11165.7(a)** (employees of a private organization whose duties require direct contact with and supervision of children; subdivision (b)'s volunteer carve-out confirming employee coverage by implication), and the sufficiency of our onboarding package: §11166.5 statement + signed acknowledgment as a condition of employment, training before first childcare shift, written 36-hour reporting procedure (§11166(a)).

**Q4 — The novel question (no direct authority found; flagged as such).** Under **Wage Order 15, §11(C)**, does the presence of a parent, household member, or client who is physically present and able to assume childcare constitute a reasonable, qualified source of relief that legally defeats the "nature of the work" defense under the ***Abdullah*** standard, requiring the cooperative to provide a standard off-duty, unpaid 30-minute meal period? Our interim policy treats a present parent as presumptive relief and restricts on-duty meal agreements to per-shift, written, paid, revocable instruments with documented justification.

**Q5 — TrustLine mandate scope.** Please confirm that the employer-of-record model sits outside Civil Code **§1812.5095**'s referral-agency TrustLine mandate (the statute's required disclosure — "[agency] is not the employer of the domestic worker it referred to you" — appearing to contemplate only the referral model, per §1812.501 definitions). Registration is our policy regardless; we want the classification on paper.

## C. Groundwork already verified (so your time goes to judgment, not retrieval)

- **Wage Order 15 §11(C)** contains only the general on-duty meal exception (written, paid, revocable). **Wage Order 5 §11(E)**'s non-revocable "sole charge" carve-out for 24-hour residential care facilities has **no Order 15 parallel** — *Palacio v. Jan & Gail's Care Homes, Inc.* (2015) 242 Cal.App.4th 1133; *L'Chaim House, Inc. v. DLSE* (2019) 38 Cal.App.5th 141 (both Order 5 cases; neither extends to Order 15).
- **Personal attendant exemption:** WO 15 §1(B) with Lab. Code §1451(d); *Cash v. Winn* (2012) 205 Cal.App.4th 1338 (20% incidental-task threshold). Our hybrids fail it by design; childcare-dominant workers will be audited per-worker via hour codes before any attendant treatment.
- **Nature-of-work test:** DLSE Op. Ltr. 2002-09-04 (multi-factor; employer bears the burden; "virtually impossible" standard); ***Abdullah v. U.S. Security Associates, Inc.***, 731 F.3d 952, 958–60 (9th Cir. 2013) (two categories; "another qualified representative reasonably available" defeats the exception); DLSE Op. Ltr. 2013-11-15 (same framework). Related DLSE letters reviewed: 2002-12-11 (split shift), 2003-11-03, 2009-06-09. *Dynabursky v. AlliedBarton* (C.D. Cal.) was examined; its retrieved orders do not contain the "sole employee" language sometimes attributed to it — we cite *Abdullah* directly and flag this so no one relies on a secondary-source misattribution.
- **Split shift:** gap >1 hr beyond a bona fide meal period; premium = one hour at the highest applicable minimum wage, offset by over-minimum pay (DLSE split-shift guidance; Op. Ltr. 2002-12-11). LA minimums as of 7/1/2026: $18.42 City / $18.47 County & Santa Monica / $20.25 West Hollywood.
- **Childcare licensing:** in-home care of one family's children in the children's own home is license-exempt (HSC §1596.792; Public Counsel license-exempt memo); Title 22 center regs inapplicable. Open org-level CDSS question being pursued separately.
- **CANRA:** Pen. Code §§11165.7, 11166, 11166.5 as summarized in Q3.
- **TrustLine:** HSC §1596.60 et seq.; CDSS PUB 439 (subsidy-triggered mandate for license-exempt providers); Civ. Code §§1812.501, 1812.5095.
